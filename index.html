iPhone 16 Series Repair Guide

iPhone 16 Mini Repair Guide (Screen & Battery)

Screen Repair

Tools Needed: Pentalobe screwdriver, Y000 screwdriver, suction cup, spudger, tweezers, new screen.

  1. Power off the phone.
  2. Remove the 2 bottom screws near the Lightning port.
  3. Place suction cup near the bottom, gently lift screen while using spudger to release clips.
  4. Open phone like a book (hinge on the side).
  5. Disconnect battery connector first.
  6. Disconnect screen connectors (usually 2–3 flat cables).
  7. Transfer front camera, Face ID, earpiece from old screen if needed.
  8. Connect new screen carefully.
  9. Reconnect battery.
  10. Test the screen before sealing.
  11. Close the phone, secure screws.

Battery Replacement

Tools Needed: Same + adhesive strips.

  1. Power off.
  2. Open screen (same steps as above).
  3. Disconnect battery connector first.
  4. Remove screws and bracket holding battery.
  5. Gently lift battery using spudger or adhesive pull tabs.
  6. Place new battery with adhesive strips.
  7. Reconnect battery connector.
  8. Test phone.
  9. Close screen and secure screws.

Mini has tiny space, so cables are fragile. Don’t force anything.

iPhone 16 & 16 Pro Repair Guide (Screen & Battery)

Screen Repair

Tools and steps are same as Mini, but screen is larger. Pro models have extra LiDAR scanner cable—disconnect carefully. Front camera and Face ID sensors must be transferred to new screen.

Battery Replacement

Larger battery than Mini, same procedure. Battery may be slightly curved; remove slowly. Pro battery connector may have extra shielding screws, keep track.

iPhone 16 Pro Max Repair Guide (Screen & Battery)

Screen Repair

Same general steps. Largest screen, so extra care for flex cables. LiDAR sensor and Pro cameras are delicate; do not bend connectors. Front assembly transfer same as Pro.

Battery Replacement

Biggest battery. Same adhesive method. Extra caution with internal screws, there are more than smaller models.

Tips for All iPhone 16 Models

  • Always disconnect battery first to avoid shorting.
  • Keep screws organized; different screws have different lengths.
  • Test everything before sealing.
  • Use plastic tools to avoid scratching internals.
  • Genuine parts = safer; aftermarket parts = cheaper but may cause errors.
  • Take pictures while opening if you forget placement.
